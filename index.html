Our Mission...

Brukner Nature Center is a non-profit, privately funded organization promoting the appreciation and understanding of wildlife conservation through preservation, education, and rehabilitation.

Welcome visitors,
We now have a convenient drop-box for admission, if you arrive before or after our normal hours to hike our trails. No pets are allowed - they scare our wildlife! Remember, there is no picking or collecting and it is important to remain on the trails!

Thank you!
-BNC Staff

Summer Art Exhibit

“The Magic of Spring”

by Nora Steele

Runs through Sun., Sept.18


Nora is a Naturalist for Wahkeena Nature Preserve and uses her illustration and photography to educate about the natural world. Her amazing art will be on sale and ready for pick-up at the end of the show. Proceeds will support the Wildlife Ambassador program at BNC.

Featured Nature Shop Item

Polymer Clay Jewelry

by Lilian Nichols


The artist of the month is Lilian Nichols, who works with polymer clay to make all sorts of jewelry - earrings, necklaces and other items. For her, polymer clay is like three dimensional paints - a perfect combination of her first two loves - painting and ceramics. Always a nature lover, her art is inspired by colors, forms and patterns found in the natural world, sometimes with a touch of whimsy. She has been working in polymer clay for about thirty years, and also teaches, spreading the fun!

Jewelry prices range from $15 - $45

A percentage of all sales will be donated back to BNC!
